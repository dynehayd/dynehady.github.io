Customer Relationship Management

Customer relationship management is the practice and strategy of which a company will analyse information to investigate why, how and when a customer will interact with the said company. The company will use this data analysis in order to achieve a higher quality service so to achieve a strengthened trust and loyalty with the customer. The customer will have left more satisfied with the transaction. This in turn will increase the growth of the business. The information is collected via website cookies, social media, staff interactions. The information is attempted to be collected from a wide variety of sources to obtain a greater understanding of the customer. Data can also be gathered through data mining; the automated extraction of hidden predictive information from large databases (Rygielski, C., 2002). This uses an algorithm to sort the useful data from the useless data. Customer relationship management systems give the staff working for an organisation the ability to access a certain customer's data. detailing previous customer behaviour, purchase history and complaints. This allows an organisation to establish solid relationships with customers based off experience with previous customers. Having a successfully implemented customer relationship management system also allows an organisation to make educated decisions on new products and services they wish to release.
